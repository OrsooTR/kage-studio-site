Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how the Kage Studio project (“Kage Studio”, “we”, “us”) handles information when you use the Kage Studio desktop application, the After Effects extension, and this website (the “Service”). We built Kage Studio to be privacy-friendly and local-first.

1. The short version

  • We do not run a central server that collects your personal data.
  • Your account (if you create one) is stored locally on your own device.
  • We do not sell your data or use advertising trackers.
  • Some features connect to third-party services (e.g. for software updates or content); those services receive technical data such as your IP address under their own privacy policies.

2. Information processed locally on your device

When you create an account in the Kage Studio desktop app, the following is stored only on your computer and is bound to your device: your chosen username, your email address, and your password in hashed form, plus a local session file. This information is not transmitted to or stored by us on any server. Your settings and preferences are likewise stored locally. You can remove all of it at any time by logging out and uninstalling the app / deleting its local data folder.

3. Connections to third-party services

To work, parts of the Service connect to third parties. We do not control these services and their handling of data is governed by their own privacy policies:

  • GitHub — used to check for and download software updates, and to host this website (GitHub Pages). GitHub may log technical request data such as your IP address.
  • Content sources — when you browse or import media, the app connects to the relevant media source(s), which may receive your IP address and request information.
  • Discord — if you choose to join our community, your use of Discord is governed by Discord’s own terms and privacy policy.
  • Google Fonts — this website loads web fonts from Google, which may receive your IP address when the page loads.

We do not set our own advertising or analytics cookies on this website. Third parties listed above may set their own cookies or process technical data as described in their policies.

4. Why we process this data (legal bases)

Where the EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) applies, our processing is based on: your consent (e.g. choosing to create a local account or join Discord); the performance of providing the Service you request (e.g. delivering updates); and our legitimate interests in keeping the Service secure and functional. Because account data stays on your device, in most cases we are not the party holding your personal data.

5. Data retention

Locally stored data remains on your device until you delete it (by logging out, removing the app, or deleting its data folder). We do not retain copies on our side.

6. Your rights

Under the GDPR and similar laws you have rights to access, rectify, erase, restrict and port your personal data, and to object to certain processing. Since your personal data is stored locally, you can exercise most of these rights directly on your own device. For anything you believe we hold, or for questions, contact us using the details below. You may also lodge a complaint with your local data-protection authority.

7. Children

The Service is not directed to children under the age required for valid consent in their country, and we do not knowingly collect personal data from them.

8. Changes

We may update this Policy from time to time; the “Last updated” date above reflects the latest version.

9. Contact

For privacy questions or requests: haah2926@gmail.com (temporary contact email).

This Policy is provided in good faith as a clear, general description of our practices. It is not legal advice; for a binding, situation-specific document, consider a review by a qualified professional.
